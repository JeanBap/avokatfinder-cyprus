Title Deed Cyprus

A title deed (titlos idioktisias) is the official document issued by the Cyprus Land Registry confirming legal ownership of immovable property.

What Is a Title Deed in Cyprus?

The title deed (Greek: titlos idioktisias) is the definitive proof of property ownership in Cyprus, issued by the Department of Lands and Surveys (DLS). It records the owner's name, the property's unique Land Registry reference, its area, location, and any encumbrances (mortgages, charges). Without a title deed in your name, you are not the legal owner — even if you have paid in full and are living in the property.

The Historic Title Deed Problem

Cyprus faced a well-documented crisis from the 1990s to the 2010s: developers sold properties to buyers, then mortgaged the underlying land to finance further construction. When developers became insolvent, the bank's mortgage on the land blocked the buyer from obtaining a title deed — even though the buyer had paid the full purchase price. Thousands of buyers were left without deeds. Legislation in 2015 (Law 9(I)/2015) and subsequent amendments partially addressed this by allowing buyers to obtain deeds despite encumbrances on the developer's land.

Checking Title Before Purchase

Before signing any contract in Cyprus, the buyer's lawyer should search the DLS register to confirm: (a) the seller is the registered owner, (b) there are no mortgages or charges that will prevent the title being transferred, and (c) no immovable property tax arrears are outstanding. This search is available at any District Lands Office and via the DLS's online portal.

Transfer of Title

Once the sale contract is signed and all monies paid, the seller must execute a transfer of ownership at the District Lands Office. Both parties (or their authorised representatives) attend, present documents, pay transfer fees, and sign the transfer. The buyer is issued a new title deed in their name — the process typically takes a few hours at the DLS if paperwork is in order.

Separate Title vs Common Title

In apartment buildings and villa complexes, buyers often encounter common title (the entire block registered under a single title) or separate title (each unit individually registered). Separate title is strongly preferable — it allows individual sale, mortgage, and inheritance without involving other owners. Buyers should always prefer property with existing separate title.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Signing a sales contract and paying in full without first checking that the title deed can actually be transferred. Developer encumbrances discovered after payment require legal proceedings to resolve.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Insist on a clean title deed search before paying any deposit. If the property has a developer's mortgage on it, ask your lawyer to confirm the mechanism for releasing it before closing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a mortgage without a title deed?

Some Cyprus banks will lend on a contract of sale, but title-deed mortgages are preferred and attract better rates.

What if my developer is insolvent and I have no deed?

Under Law 9(I)/2015, buyers can apply to the DLS to transfer despite developer encumbrances — engage a Cyprus property lawyer experienced in this area.

How long does the transfer take?

If paperwork is in order and no encumbrances, the physical transfer at the DLS takes 1–3 hours on the appointment day.
