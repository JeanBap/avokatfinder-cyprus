Capital Gains Tax Cyprus

Cyprus Capital Gains Tax (CGT) is levied at 20% on gains from the disposal of immovable property situated in Cyprus, with a lifetime exemption of €17,086 for primary residences.

Overview of Cyprus CGT

Cyprus levies Capital Gains Tax (CGT) on gains arising from the disposal of immovable property situated in Cyprus. The tax rate is 20% of the net gain. CGT is the seller's obligation and is calculated and paid to the Tax Department at or before the time of transfer. The notional gain is the sale price minus the indexed acquisition cost minus allowable expenditure.

Calculating the Gain

The taxable gain is calculated as: (net proceeds) minus (indexed acquisition cost) minus (allowable expenses). The acquisition cost is indexed for inflation using the Consumer Price Index from the date of acquisition to the date of disposal. Allowable expenses include documented improvement costs, transfer fees paid on acquisition, and professional fees related to the acquisition. The index adjustment alone can significantly reduce the nominal gain — for properties held more than 10 years, this reduction is often substantial.

Lifetime Exemptions

Cyprus allows several lifetime CGT exemptions: a general lifetime allowance of €17,086 per individual on the sale of their primary residence (provided conditions are met — owner must have lived there for at least 5 years); a further €25,629 lifetime exemption on gains from agricultural land sold by a farmer; and €85,430 for gains on property sold where the proceeds are used to purchase another primary residence within two years. These exemptions can be accumulated.

Exemptions from CGT

Certain disposals are fully exempt from CGT: property transferred as part of a gift to a direct-line family member (spouse, child, parent) or to a relative within two degrees (brother, sister); transfers on death (inheritance); and transfers pursuant to a court order in matrimonial proceedings. Company restructuring transfers may also qualify under specific provisions.

Non-Resident Sellers

Non-resident sellers are subject to Cyprus CGT on Cyprus property disposals at the same 20% rate. The tax must be paid before the DLS will register the transfer. Double tax treaties may provide a credit against home-country tax on the same gain — non-residents should take advice from a Cyprus tax adviser well in advance of the sale to understand the net position.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Selling without indexing the acquisition cost. Many sellers overestimate their CGT liability because they use the nominal purchase price rather than the CPI-adjusted figure.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Keep all original purchase documentation, including stamp duty receipts and improvement invoices, from the day you buy — you will need them years later to support the CGT calculation when you sell.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is CGT the same as stamp duty?

No — CGT is the seller's tax on the gain; stamp duty is the buyer's tax on the purchase price.

Can CGT be deferred?

Not in the way some other jurisdictions offer rollover relief — Cyprus CGT is payable on disposal, though the reinvestment exemption is available for primary residences.

What if I donate the property rather than sell it?

Gifts to direct-line family members are exempt from CGT but the recipient takes on the donor's acquisition cost basis.
