Property Valuation Cyprus

A property valuation in Cyprus is conducted by a certified valuer to determine market value for mortgage purposes, tax assessments, or to inform purchase negotiations.

Who Conducts Valuations?

Cyprus property valuations are conducted by certified valuers (registered with the Cyprus Scientific and Technical Chamber — ETEK, or the Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors — RICS) and by bank-appointed valuers for mortgage purposes. Banks commission their own independent valuation before approving a loan — buyers cannot use a valuation they commissioned themselves.

Methods Used

Valuers in Cyprus typically use three approaches: the sales comparison approach (comparing with recent comparable sales in the area), the income approach (for rental investment properties — capitalising the net rental income), and the cost approach (replacement cost minus depreciation, used for specialist buildings). Residential properties primarily use the sales comparison method.

DLS Assessed Value

The Department of Lands and Surveys uses its own internal assessment of market value for transfer fee and CGT calculation purposes. This DLS value may differ from the independent valuation and from the contract price. Buyers and sellers should be aware that the DLS can substitute its own value if the declared sale price appears below market.

When to Get a Valuation

A buyer should commission an independent pre-purchase valuation when: the asking price appears high relative to comparables; the property has features that are difficult to price (cliff-top views, unusual architecture); or when buying at auction. For resales where the bank commissions a valuation anyway, buyers sometimes rely on the bank's valuer — be aware the bank valuation is primarily for the bank's security, not the buyer's information.

Fees

Valuation fees in Cyprus range from around €300–€800 for a standard residential property, depending on complexity and the valuer's firm. RICS-registered firms typically charge at the higher end but provide reports that are internationally recognised for cross-border lending and dispute purposes.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming the bank's valuation confirms the property is worth the asking price. The bank's valuer confirms minimum security value — not that the price is fair market value.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For any property above €300,000, commission your own RICS-accredited valuation independently of the bank's — it costs €500 and can save tens of thousands in overpayment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I appeal the DLS assessed value?

Yes — lodge a written objection within the prescribed period; the DLS will review with supporting comparables.

Is a RICS valuation accepted by all Cyprus banks?

Most major Cypriot banks accept RICS valuations for mortgage purposes.

How current does the valuation need to be?

Banks typically require a valuation no older than 3–6 months at the time of mortgage drawdown.
