Immovable Property Tax Cyprus

Cyprus abolished its annual immovable property tax in 2017, but buyers should be aware of legacy arrears, municipality levies, and sewerage board charges that still apply.

Abolition of the Annual IPT

Cyprus's Immovable Property Tax (IPT) — formerly levied annually by the state on the 1980 assessed value of all property — was abolished as of 1 January 2017. This was a significant reform: prior to abolition, high-value properties faced substantial annual tax bills. Buyers in Cyprus are no longer subject to an annual state IPT, which removes a meaningful ongoing cost compared to many EU neighbours.

Legacy Arrears

Although the tax is abolished, arrears that accumulated before 2017 remain payable. A property with unpaid historical IPT is encumbered — the DLS can refuse to register a transfer until arrears are settled. Buyers should confirm with the seller that all IPT arrears up to 2016 have been paid in full before signing the contract of sale. Confirmation can be obtained from the Tax Department.

Municipal Levies

While the state IPT is gone, municipalities in Cyprus charge their own annual levies for refuse collection, street cleaning, and local services. These are set by each municipality and are modest in amount, typically €100–€500 per year for residential properties. The seller should provide proof that municipal levies are paid up to date — unpaid amounts can become a charge on the property.

Sewerage Board Charges

Properties connected to the sewerage system pay annual sewerage board charges. These vary by district (administered by separate district sewerage boards) and are calculated on a formula based on the property's assessed value or usage. Unpaid sewerage charges can also create a priority debt on the property. Confirm their status before exchange.

Capital Gains Tax

Although there is no annual property tax, sellers in Cyprus are subject to Capital Gains Tax (CGT) of 20% on gains from the disposal of immovable property situated in Cyprus. CGT is payable by the seller on the actual gain (proceeds minus acquisition cost minus allowable expenses). An exemption of €17,086 per individual per lifetime applies for gains on the disposal of a primary residence (subject to conditions). Non-resident sellers are equally subject to Cyprus CGT on Cyprus property gains.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming no property-related taxes apply in Cyprus because IPT was abolished. Municipal levies, sewerage charges, and seller CGT all still apply.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask for a Tax Department clearance certificate confirming zero IPT arrears as a condition precedent in the contract of sale — this is a standard request your lawyer should make.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there an annual property tax in Cyprus today?

No state IPT — abolished in 2017. Municipal and sewerage charges remain.

Who pays CGT — buyer or seller?

CGT is the seller's obligation. The buyer pays transfer fees and stamp duty.

Can I offset renovation costs against CGT?

Yes — documented improvement costs are deductible from the gain, reducing the CGT liability.
