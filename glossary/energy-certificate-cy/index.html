Energy Performance Certificate Cyprus

An Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) is mandatory in Cyprus for all residential property sales and rentals, grading the building's energy efficiency from A to G.

Legal Requirement

Cyprus implemented the EU Energy Performance of Buildings Directive through the Energy Performance of Buildings Law. An Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) — known locally as the Pistopoiitiko Energiakis Apodosis — is mandatory for all residential and commercial buildings when sold or rented. It must be shown to prospective buyers or tenants and a copy annexed to the sales contract or tenancy agreement.

Rating Scale

Cyprus EPCs are rated from A (most energy efficient) through to G (least efficient), based on primary energy consumption per square metre per year. The rating reflects the building's thermal envelope (insulation, windows), heating and cooling systems, hot water provision, and lighting. New buildings must achieve at a minimum a B+ rating under current regulations.

Who Issues the Certificate?

EPCs must be issued by accredited energy assessors registered with the Energy Service of the Ministry of Energy. Buyers should verify the assessor's accreditation and the certificate's expiry date (EPCs are valid for 10 years). A certificate purchased from a previous owner's sale may still be valid — check the issue date.

Impact on Transactions

A poor EPC rating (E, F, or G) does not legally prevent a sale or rental in Cyprus currently, but it signals high energy costs and potential renovation need. Buyers and tenants are increasingly factoring in EPC ratings, particularly for properties where utility bills are high year-round due to Mediterranean summer cooling loads. Some banks are starting to offer green mortgage products with preferential rates for A and B-rated properties.

Renovation and Grants

Cyprus's government periodically offers energy efficiency grant schemes (Save and Upgrade type programmes, often EU-funded) for property owners wishing to improve their EPC rating. Insulation, solar thermal, heat pump, and PV installations typically qualify. Buyers of poorly-rated properties should research current grant availability — it can offset a significant portion of renovation cost.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Accepting a 10-year-old EPC from the seller without checking the issue date. An expired certificate is not legally compliant and must be renewed before sale.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: For properties with split A/C cooling (the majority of Cyprus homes), check whether the EPC assessor properly modelled the cooling load — some assessors underestimate Mediterranean cooling energy use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the EPC the same as a structural survey?

No — the EPC covers energy performance only. A structural survey covers physical condition.

Can the EPC be used for negotiation?

Yes — a low rating is legitimate grounds for a price reduction or seller-funded energy improvements as a condition of sale.

Does EPC cover solar panels already installed?

Yes — existing PV and solar thermal systems are factored into the assessment and improve the rating.
