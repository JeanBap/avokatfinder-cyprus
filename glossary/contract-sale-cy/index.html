Contract of Sale Cyprus

The contract of sale is the binding agreement between buyer and seller in Cyprus, which must be deposited at the District Lands Office within 60 days to be enforceable against third parties.

The Importance of Depositing the Contract

In Cyprus, a contract of sale (synallagi agoras) is only fully protected against third-party claims — including a seller's creditors or a second buyer — if it is deposited (stamped and filed) at the District Lands Office (DLS) within 60 days of signing. This is a distinctive feature of Cyprus property law. An undeposited contract gives the buyer contractual rights against the seller but does not protect against the seller mortgaging or reselling the property.

What Must Be in the Contract

A compliant Cyprus contract of sale must include: full identification of the parties, a description of the property by land registry reference, the agreed purchase price and payment schedule, any suspensive conditions (mortgage, planning), a delivery date for off-plan property, and warranties as to title. The contract is typically in both Greek and English. It must be stamped by the Tax Commissioner and the stamp duty paid before deposit.

Stamp Duty

Cyprus stamp duty on contracts of sale is levied at 0.15% on the first €170,860 of the purchase price and 0.2% on the excess. It is payable within 30 days of signing the contract. Late payment attracts a 10% penalty plus interest. The buyer typically pays stamp duty, though the contract may allocate it differently.

Off-Plan Contracts

For off-plan (under construction) properties, the contract should include a detailed specification of the unit, the developer's obligation to obtain planning permission, the construction timeline, and delay penalty provisions. Deposit the contract immediately after signing — do not wait for the property to be completed. An undeposited off-plan contract is extremely vulnerable if the developer encounters financial difficulties.

Specific Performance

A deposited contract of sale in Cyprus can be enforced by specific performance — a court order compelling the seller to complete the transfer. This is a powerful remedy that makes the deposited contract effectively equivalent to a real property right. It is one reason why timely deposit is so critical: undeposited contracts can only give rise to damages claims, not specific performance.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Paying a large deposit and then delaying contract deposit for months. If the developer or seller is declared insolvent before the contract is deposited, your position is dramatically weakened.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Instruct your lawyer to deposit the stamped contract within the first week after signing — don't wait until close to the 60-day deadline. The process is straightforward and the protection is irreplaceable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who physically deposits the contract?

Your Cyprus lawyer or the notary — it is a standard part of a Cypriot conveyance.

Can I sell the property before getting a title deed?

Yes — you can assign your contract of sale to a new buyer, subject to the original contract's terms and the seller's consent.

Does deposit protect me if the seller mortgages the land?

Yes — a deposited contract has priority over subsequently registered mortgages; an undeposited one does not.
