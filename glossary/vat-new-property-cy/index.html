VAT on New Property Cyprus

New residential properties in Cyprus are subject to 19% VAT, but first-time buyers purchasing a primary residence can apply for a reduced 5% rate on the first 200 square metres.

VAT on New Construction in Cyprus

Cyprus levies VAT at the standard rate of 19% on the sale of newly developed residential properties (properties being sold for the first time after construction). This applies to apartments, houses, and villas sold by a developer-vendor. Resale properties (sold by private individuals who are not in the business of property development) are generally not subject to VAT — only transfer fees and stamp duty apply.

The 5% Reduced Rate

A significantly reduced VAT rate of 5% applies to the acquisition of a dwelling intended for use as the buyer's primary and permanent residence in Cyprus, subject to specific conditions: the buyer must be an individual (not a company), must not have previously benefited from the reduced rate, and the reduced rate applies only to the first 200 m² of covered area. For properties above 200 m², the first 200 m² is taxed at 5% and the excess at 19%.

Application Process

The reduced rate is not automatic — it requires an application to the Tax Department with supporting documentation: a statutory declaration that the property will be the buyer's primary residence, evidence of first-time buyer status, and details of the property. The application should be submitted before or at the time of purchase. The buyer must occupy the property within one year of purchase and maintain it as their primary residence for at least 10 years — failure to do so triggers a claw-back of the VAT benefit.

VAT vs Transfer Fee Trade-off

New properties subject to VAT are exempt from transfer fees. This creates a comparison: 19% VAT on a new property vs. ~8% transfer fees on an equivalent resale property. Even with the 5% reduced rate for first-time buyers, the arithmetic depends on the property value and area. A tax adviser should model both scenarios — the result is not always obvious.

Developer VAT Recovery

Developers who charge VAT to buyers recover the VAT they paid on construction inputs through the Cyprus VAT system. Buyers who receive a 5% VAT invoice instead of 19% should ensure the developer issues a proper VAT invoice — this matters for the Tax Department's records and for the buyer's claw-back liability if they later breach the residency conditions.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying a new 250m² property and expecting 5% VAT on the entire area. The reduced rate applies only to the first 200m² — the extra 50m² is taxed at 19%.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Submit the 5% VAT application to the Tax Department before or at the signing date — late applications are rejected and you revert to the 19% rate with no recourse.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a non-Cypriot get the 5% VAT rate?

Yes — citizenship is not a condition. The property must be the buyer's primary residence in Cyprus.

What if I sell within 10 years?

The Tax Department will assess a proportional claw-back of the VAT benefit based on how much of the 10-year period has elapsed.

Does the 5% rate apply to land and construction separately?

No — VAT is charged on the total property price. The land component forms part of the taxable base.
