Transfer Fees Cyprus

Transfer fees in Cyprus are levied by the Department of Lands and Surveys when ownership of a property is officially transferred, calculated as a percentage of the market value.

What Are Transfer Fees?

Cyprus property transfer fees are charged by the Department of Lands and Surveys (DLS) when a property changes hands. They are calculated on the property's assessed market value (which the DLS determines, not the declared sale price) and are paid at the time of the physical transfer at the District Lands Office. The buyer is liable for transfer fees in standard transactions.

Standard Fee Scale

The fee scale as of recent years is: 3% on the first €85,430 of assessed value; 5% on the next €85,430 (i.e. up to €170,860); and 8% on the balance above €170,860. For example, on a property assessed at €300,000, the total transfer fee is approximately €13,500. These rates can change with government policy — verify the current schedule with your lawyer at the time of purchase.

Exemptions and Reductions

Cyprus has periodically offered transfer fee exemptions or reductions to stimulate the market. Notably, VAT-subject new properties (where the developer charges 19% VAT on the purchase price) are fully exempt from transfer fees. This exemption can make brand-new properties cheaper in terms of transaction costs despite the headline VAT charge. Additionally, transfers between family members in direct line attract reduced or zero transfer fees.

VAT on New Properties

First-time buyers of new residential property in Cyprus can apply for a reduced VAT rate of 5% (instead of the standard 19%) on the first 200 m² of a property intended as their primary residence. This is a significant saving — on a €400,000 property, the difference between 5% and 19% VAT is €56,000. The reduced rate requires an application and certification of first-time buyer status.

Planning and Timing

Because transfer fees are assessed on the DLS's market value rather than the contract price, a low declared price does not reduce the fee. Budget transfer fees based on the realistic market value. For phased payments over a long period (off-plan), the assessment is made at the time of transfer, not at contract date — market value changes during construction affect the final fee.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming the transfer fee is based on the declared price in the contract. The DLS assesses its own market value independently, which may be higher than the agreed sale price.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: If buying a new property from a developer who charges VAT, confirm the transfer fee exemption applies — this changes the total cost calculation significantly and affects whether second-hand vs new is cheaper.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I negotiate the DLS's market value assessment?

Yes — you can lodge an objection if you believe the assessed value is above market. Your lawyer can advise on the objection process.

Are transfer fees the same in Limassol and Paphos?

Yes — transfer fees are set nationally by law, not by district.

What if I buy at auction?

Transfer fees apply equally to auction purchases based on the hammer price or DLS assessment, whichever is higher.
