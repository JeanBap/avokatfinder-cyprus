Rental Yield Cyprus

Rental yield measures the annual return a Cyprus investment property generates relative to its purchase price, a key metric for property investors evaluating buy-to-let opportunities.

How Rental Yield Is Calculated

Gross rental yield = (annual gross rent ÷ purchase price) × 100. For example, a property purchased for €200,000 renting for €1,200/month generates a gross yield of 7.2%. Net yield deducts vacancy allowance, management fees, maintenance costs, property taxes, and insurance from the annual rent before dividing — typically reducing gross yield by 2–3 percentage points for Cyprus.

Typical Yields in Cyprus

Short-term holiday lets in Paphos and Limassol tourist areas can achieve gross yields of 7–12% in peak seasons, but net yields after MTA licence fees, property management (15–20% of revenue), cleaning, and off-season vacancy typically settle at 4–6%. Long-term residential rentals in urban areas (Limassol, Nicosia, Larnaca) achieve steadier gross yields of 4–6%, with lower management costs and vacancy risk.

Factors Affecting Yield

Key variables that affect Cyprus rental yield include: location (seafront and marina properties command premium short-term rents but higher prices reduce yield); property type (apartments outperform villas on yield due to lower price per bedroom); management arrangement (self-managed vs professional agency); and seasonality (peak season rates in Paphos are 3–5x off-season rates, creating uneven cash flow).

Tax on Rental Income

Rental income in Cyprus is subject to income tax for individuals (at rates up to 35%) and to Special Defence Contribution (SDC) at 3% for residents. Non-residents are subject to income tax but not SDC. Allowable deductions include management fees, maintenance costs, depreciation, and mortgage interest (for properties not subject to capital allowances). A Cyprus tax adviser should model the net-of-tax yield before investment decisions are made.

Capital Growth vs Income Return

Cyprus property investors face a choice between income-focused investment (strong yield, moderate growth — typically long-term residential in provincial areas) and growth-focused investment (lower yield, higher appreciation potential — prime Limassol seafront, marina, and lifestyle properties). Many investors seek a balance — properties with 4–5% net yield plus realistic 3–5% annual capital appreciation provide total returns comparable to other Mediterranean markets.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Comparing gross yield figures from agent marketing materials without deducting management fees, vacancy, and tax. The net yield can be half the advertised gross.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Model a 12-week low-season void when evaluating a Paphos holiday let — many first-time investors underestimate off-season vacancy and find the annual net return disappointing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Airbnb legal in Cyprus?

Yes — with a valid MTA licence for tourist accommodation. Unlicensed short-term lets are subject to fines.

Are there property management companies in Cyprus?

Yes — particularly in Limassol, Paphos, and Ayia Napa, with English-language services widely available.

Can I deduct mortgage interest from rental income?

For individuals, limited deductibility applies — confirm current rules with a Cyprus tax adviser as the treatment has evolved.
