Cyprus Residency by Investment

Cyprus offers permanent residency to non-EU nationals who invest in qualifying real estate, under a programme administered by the Civil Registry and Migration Department.

Overview of the Programme

Cyprus's Permanent Residency by Investment programme (administered by the Civil Registry and Migration Department) allows non-EU nationals to obtain Category F immigration permits — permanent residency — in exchange for qualifying real estate investment. The programme is popular with investors from Russia, China, the Middle East, and other non-EU countries who seek EU-based residency rights.

Investment Requirements

The primary route requires the purchase of one or two new residential properties (from a development company) with a combined market value of at least €300,000 (excluding VAT). The property must be purchased new — resale properties do not qualify. The applicant must make a deposit of at least €30,000 into a Cypriot bank account and maintain it for at least 3 years. Proof of secured annual income from abroad (minimum €30,000 per year, plus €5,000 per dependent) is also required.

What Residency Gives You

The Category F permanent residency permit is for life and is not conditional on continued property ownership after the initial minimum period. However, the permit does not automatically give the right to work in Cyprus (a separate work permit is required for employment). It allows the holder to live in Cyprus indefinitely, travel freely within Cyprus, and — importantly — it does not confer Schengen freedom of movement, as Cyprus is not currently in the Schengen Area.

Due Diligence and Application

The Civil Registry and Migration Department conducts due diligence on applicants, including source of funds checks. The application includes certified documents, bank statements, police clearance certificates, and health insurance evidence. Processing times have varied from 2 to 9 months. Using a qualified Cypriot immigration lawyer to prepare the application significantly reduces rejection risk.

Post-Pandemic Updates

Cyprus's Citizenship by Investment (golden passport) programme was suspended in 2020 following media and EU scrutiny. The residency programme remains active but rules and processing times have evolved. Verify current requirements with the Civil Registry or a qualified immigration adviser — do not rely on information more than 12 months old given the frequency of rule changes.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Confusing permanent residency with citizenship. The Category F permit gives residency rights, not a Cyprus passport or EU citizenship — and does not include Schengen freedom of movement.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Buy a new property from a developer specifically participating in the residency programme — not all developer sales qualify, and the 'new' requirement is strictly interpreted by the Civil Registry.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Cyprus in Schengen?

Not yet — Cyprus is an EU member but has not joined the Schengen Area, so residency does not give Schengen travel rights.

Can I rent out the qualifying property?

Yes — there is no restriction on renting after purchase, though you must maintain the qualifying investment as evidence of compliance.

How long does the permit last?

Category F is a permanent permit — it does not expire, though re-entry requirements apply if you are absent from Cyprus for an extended period.
