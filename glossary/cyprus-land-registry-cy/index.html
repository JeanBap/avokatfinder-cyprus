Cyprus Land Registry

The Cyprus Department of Lands and Surveys (DLS) maintains the Land Registry, recording all property ownership, encumbrances, and transfers across the Republic of Cyprus.

Role of the Department of Lands and Surveys

The Department of Lands and Surveys (DLS) — in Greek, Tmima Ktimatoloigiou kai Choromitrias — is Cyprus's central land authority. It maintains the Land Registry (recording ownership, mortgages, and encumbrances for every parcel in the Republic of Cyprus), issues title deeds, handles property transfers, and conducts the official cadastral survey. All land transactions in Cyprus must pass through the DLS.

District Land Offices

The DLS operates district offices in Nicosia, Limassol, Larnaca, Paphos, and Famagusta (for government-controlled areas). Property-specific matters — transfers, mortgage registrations, title deed searches — are handled at the district office corresponding to the property's location. Your lawyer will interact with the relevant district office on your behalf.

Title Deed Search

A DLS title search reveals the current registered owner, the property's Land Registry reference (sheet/plan/parcel/block numbers), area, any registered mortgages or charges (hypothecs), easements, and tax clearance status. Searches are available electronically via the DLS's online portal and in person at district offices. The search is a snapshot — order a fresh one close to the transfer date to catch last-minute encumbrances.

Registration of Encumbrances

Mortgages (hypothecs) must be registered at the DLS to be enforceable against third parties. Priority among competing mortgages is by date of registration. When a mortgage is discharged, a formal release (apeleutherotiko) must be registered at the DLS to clear the title. Buyers should confirm that the seller's mortgage release has been registered — not merely that the loan has been repaid.

Immovable Property Information System (IPIS)

The DLS operates an online Immovable Property Information System where members of the public can search property details, view ownership information (with limitations for privacy), and verify title status for registered parcels. While useful as a first check, professional advice and a full official search are still required before any transaction.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Assuming a verbal assurance from the seller that their mortgage is paid off. A mortgage must be formally discharged at the DLS — a repaid loan with no registered release still shows as an encumbrance.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Run a DLS search yourself via the IPIS portal as a first check, then commission a full official search through your lawyer before exchange. The two-step process is quick and catches obvious issues early.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I search the DLS online?

Yes — the IPIS portal provides basic ownership information. Full certified searches are done at the district office.

What if a property's title is still in the developer's name?

This is the historical title deed problem — Law 9(I)/2015 provides a mechanism to transfer despite developer encumbrances.

How do I find my property's Land Registry reference?

It appears in the contract of sale and on the original title deed. If you don't have these, your lawyer can search by address.
