Planning Permission Cyprus

Planning permission (adeias oikodomis) from the local municipality or district administration is required in Cyprus before any construction or significant alteration to an existing building.

The Two-Permit System

Cyprus uses a two-stage permit system for construction: a planning permit (politiki adeia) and a building permit (oikodomiki adeia). The planning permit authorises the use and mass of a proposed development in relation to the zoning plan. The building permit — issued after the planning permit — approves the detailed structural and mechanical design. Both are required before construction may commence.

Who Issues Permits?

Responsibility for planning and building permits sits with the district's technical services department (techniki ypiresia), which is part of the relevant municipality or the government's district administration for rural areas. In Nicosia, Limassol, Larnaca, Famagusta, and Paphos districts, different offices handle different types of development. Large or environmentally sensitive projects may also require Environmental Impact Assessment approval from the Department of Environment.

Density and Height Limits

Each zone in Cyprus's Local Development Plan sets maximum floor area ratios (sytelestis domisis) and building height limits. These determine how much can be built on a given plot. Buyers purchasing land for development must confirm the permitted density — a plot of 1,000 m² might only permit 400 m² of built floor area depending on the zone and local plan provisions.

Swimming Pools and Extensions

Even residential additions like swimming pools, carports, and garden structures above certain sizes require a planning and building permit in Cyprus. Many properties have unpermitted pools or extensions — a widespread issue. Buyers who discover unpermitted structures take on the risk of enforcement, which can include fines and demolition orders.

Regularisation

Cyprus has run periodic regularisation (amnesties) for unpermitted structures, allowing owners to pay a fine and regularise their building. However, regularisation is not available for all types of violations and is not guaranteed — it depends on whether the structure could have been permitted if properly applied for. Do not buy assuming regularisation will be available as a safety net.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Buying a property with a swimming pool that has no permit, assuming it 'must be fine' because the neighbours have one too. Unpermitted pools are the subject of enforcement notices across Cyprus.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Before buying any property with structures beyond the main house, commission a licensed architect's report confirming all structures are covered by valid permits or eligible for regularisation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a planning permit take?

Simple residential: 2–6 months; complex or environmentally sensitive: 12+ months.

Can I build on agricultural land?

Agricultural land (zone AZ) is very restricted — a limited number of dwellings per hectare may be permitted under specific conditions.

What is the fine for building without a permit?

Fines vary but structural violations can attract significant penalties plus mandatory remediation or demolition orders.
